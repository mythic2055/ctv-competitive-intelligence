# U.S. CTV Advertising Market Baseline (Jan 2024 - Mar 2026)

**Prepared:** March 1, 2026  
**Scope:** U.S. connected TV (CTV) advertising market, including demand growth, viewing shifts, measurement infrastructure, and supply-path signals relevant to performance advertisers.

## Method and Evidence Standard
This baseline uses a source hierarchy consistent with the execution plan:
1. SEC filings and public-company investor disclosures
2. Official research and organization/company publications (IAB, Nielsen, IR releases, product announcements)
3. Reputable trade media (used only when primary sources are unavailable)

For this first baseline, the majority of claims come from IAB, Nielsen, SEC filings, and investor materials. Company self-reported claims are explicitly labeled.

## Executive Baseline
- U.S. ad spend is still expanding, with IAB forecasting **+9.5% in 2026**, while CTV is projected to grow faster at **+13.8%**. [S1]
- Digital video is now the central growth engine in TV/video budgets: IAB estimates digital video at **$64.4B (2024)** and **$72.4B (2025 projected)**, with CTV the largest format segment. [S2]
- Audience behavior has structurally shifted: Nielsen reported streaming at **44.8% of TV viewing in May 2025** and **47.5% in Dec 2025**. [S3][S4]
- The market is increasingly ad-funded: ad-supported viewing represented **72.9% of TV usage in Q3 2025** and peaked at **74.7% in September 2025**. [S5]
- Measurement is becoming a hard buying requirement: IAB buyer survey data shows rising emphasis on cross-platform measurement and broader CAPI adoption to improve ROAS and targeting precision. [S1][S6]
- Competitive structure is moving toward direct, transparent CTV paths (publisher-direct and supply-path optimization claims), reflected in recent platform launches and integrations. [S13][S17]

## 1) Demand and Spend Trajectory
IAB's December 2025 outlook points to broad ad-market resilience with 2026 U.S. ad spend expected to rise **9.5%**, while CTV outpaces the market at **13.8% growth**. Linear TV is expected to contract (**-1.7%**), reinforcing a structural migration from legacy TV toward digital video channels. [S1]

IAB's digital video report shows the medium-term spend shift clearly:
- Digital video ad spend: **$64.4B in 2024**, projected **$72.4B in 2025**
- CTV ad spend: **$20.3B (2023)** -> **$23.6B (2024)** -> **$26.6B (2025 projected)**
- 2024 CTV growth rebounded to **+16% YoY**
- Social video and online video remain major complements at **$23.7B** and **$16.6B** respectively in 2024 [S2]

The same IAB report indicates where incremental CTV dollars are expected to come from in 2025: substantial reallocation from both linear TV and social channels (each cited at **36%** of growth contribution in the report). This indicates CTV is no longer only a "TV substitute," but also a performance reallocation destination inside digital budget portfolios. [S2]

## 2) Viewing Behavior and Ad Supply Fundamentals
Nielsen data confirms the audience-side foundation for continued CTV investment.

In **May 2025**, streaming reached **44.8%** of total TV viewing, exceeding the combined broadcast+cable share (**44.2%**) for the first time. Within the same month, broadcast was **20.1%** and cable **24.1%**, with streaming usage up **71%** versus May 2021. [S3]

By **December 2025**, streaming reached a new high of **47.5%** of TV usage; broadcast and cable declined to **21.4%** and **20.2%** respectively. Nielsen also reported record-high streaming activity during holiday periods and growing FAST participation (for example, Roku Channel share reaching **3.0%** in December). [S4]

Ad availability appears durable because viewing growth is increasingly ad-funded rather than subscription-only. Nielsen's ad-supported report states ad-supported content accounted for **72.9%** of TV viewing in Q3 2025, with a **74.7%** high in September. [S5]

Implication: CTV inventory depth is improving not only through audience migration, but through expansion of ad-supported consumption environments.

## 3) Measurement and Attribution Infrastructure
Spend migration is increasingly gated by measurement confidence.

IAB's 2026 outlook reports that **72%** of buyers prioritize cross-platform audience measurement (up from **64%** in the prior year), showing that identity resolution and deduplicated reach/frequency have become core budget prerequisites. [S1]

IAB's CAPI guide adds tactical detail:
- Around **two-thirds** of surveyed advertisers report improved ROAS after CAPI implementation
- **92%** report enriched audience targeting signals
- Major blockers remain, including technical complexity and compliance friction among publishers/media owners [S6]

This supports a practical conclusion: the next wave of CTV share capture will depend less on top-funnel pitch narratives and more on demonstrable measurement and optimization workflows (conversion APIs, event quality controls, and transparent reporting).

## 4) Supply-Path and Platform Dynamics
Recent platform announcements suggest a continued shift toward direct and more controllable CTV supply paths.

- Viant announced direct-access expansion in CTV via Magnite SpringServe integration, signaling the strategic premium on direct publisher connectivity and reduced path complexity. [S13]
- Upstream (rebrand from TheViewPoint) launched direct-sales automation for CTV publishers, explicitly framing direct CTV buying as an efficiency and control opportunity. [S17]

This direction aligns with broader buyer pressure for quality inventory, cost transparency, and better measurement-to-activation loops.

## 5) Competitive Structure Signals (Early Read)
Initial public and company-released indicators suggest performance CTV competition is intensifying around self-serve usability, measurable outcomes, and supply-path access:

- MNTN's SEC filing reports **$290.093M** in 2025 revenue, sharply narrower net loss, and a strong cash position with no borrowings. [S8]
- Viant's Q3 2025 release states CTV accounted for **46%** of its total ad spend and CTV spend grew **45% YoY**, underscoring the centrality of CTV within independent adtech platforms. [S12]
- Pinterest's acquisition of tvScientific (announced in 2025 and closed Feb 17, 2026 per SEC filing) indicates broader performance-media platforms are adding native CTV capability. [S14][S15]
- Tatari's September 2025 platform update and Upstream's February 2026 launch both emphasize convergent TV workflows and direct CTV purchasing mechanics. [S16][S17]
- Vibe's September 2025 financing round and growth claims indicate continued capital support for performance-oriented CTV challengers. (Self-reported metrics.) [S18]

## 6) Contradictions, Risks, and Evidence Caveats
- **Self-reported performance metrics:** Several claims (for private companies and vendor-issued releases) are not independently audited; treat as directional until corroborated.
- **Taxonomy differences:** "CTV," "streaming TV," and "digital video" are not perfectly interchangeable across datasets.
- **Attribution variance:** ROAS/incrementality outcomes depend heavily on event instrumentation quality and model assumptions.
- **Timing artifacts:** Seasonal events (e.g., holiday streaming spikes) can distort short-window comparisons.
