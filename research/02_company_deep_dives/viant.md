# Viant Deep-Dive (Step 2)

**Company under analysis:** Viant Technology  
**Peer anchors:** MNTN and Tatari  
**Window:** Jan 1, 2024 to Mar 1, 2026

## 1) Executive Summary
- Viant is a public adtech platform with a strong CTV concentration and increasing emphasis on AI-enabled buying and direct supply-path quality.[1][2][3]
- In Q3 2025, Viant reported CTV represented 46% of total ad spend on its platform and CTV spend grew 45% YoY, confirming CTV as a central growth pillar.[1]
- Viant’s launch of ViantAI and related automation tooling points to a strategy of algorithmic optimization across omnichannel inventory, including CTV.[2]
- Supply strategy is increasingly explicit: Viant announced direct-access expansion with Magnite SpringServe, signaling focus on quality and efficiency in CTV execution paths.[3]
- Compared with MNTN, Viant’s advantage is broader omnichannel DSP context and public operating scale; MNTN’s advantage is sharper performance-TV brand specialization for growth advertisers.[4]
- Compared with Tatari, Viant appears stronger in public financial transparency and broad DSP platform scope; Tatari emphasizes convergent TV and direct-buying infrastructure narrative.[5]
- Viant’s public-company reporting improves confidence on durability metrics versus private peers.
- Core competitive risk: large independent platforms (TTD), plus focused performance CTV specialists (MNTN, Tatari) can pressure Viant from both ends of the market.
- Viant’s next 12–24 months likely hinge on sustaining AI performance gains while translating direct-supply initiatives into measurable advertiser outcomes.
- Key uncertainty remains around relative win-rate by segment (SMB vs agency vs enterprise) in CTV-specific deals.

## 2) Company Profile
Viant operates as an AI-powered DSP/platform across channels, with CTV occupying an increasingly material share of platform activity.[1][2] Its public disclosure cadence (earnings releases + SEC filings) provides stronger observability than private competitors.

Viant’s ICP spans agencies and advertisers requiring scaled programmatic infrastructure and cross-channel execution. In CTV, Viant appears focused on performance and measurable outcomes while preserving omnichannel breadth.

## 3) Product and Technical Analysis
### Platform and AI
ViantAI (announced in 2025) reflects an explicit push toward AI-assisted planning, bidding, and optimization workflows.[2] This aligns with broader market movement toward algorithmic media buying and operational automation.

### Measurement and outcomes
Viant’s investor materials emphasize measurable media outcomes and optimization features, but public source detail on standardized incrementality methodology remains less granular than some buyers may require for strict apples-to-apples comparisons.

### Workflow depth
As an established DSP, Viant can bundle CTV with broader channel orchestration, which can be an advantage for advertisers managing omnichannel budgets under unified governance.

## 4) Supply-Path Analysis
Viant’s August 2025 direct-access expansion with Magnite SpringServe is a notable signal: the company is explicitly pursuing more direct CTV routes to improve inventory quality and reduce unnecessary path complexity.[3]

Inference: this strategy helps defend performance outcomes as buyers scrutinize fee transparency and inventory provenance in CTV.

## 5) GTM Strategy
Viant’s GTM appears to leverage:
- Public-company trust and operational scale
- Omnichannel execution across advertiser categories
- AI-forward productivity narrative
- Growing CTV concentration as a strategic growth vector

This creates a differentiated posture versus narrower CTV-first challengers while preserving appeal to agencies seeking one platform for broader media execution.

## 6) Commercial and Financial Signals
From official earnings materials and SEC reporting references:
- Q3 2025 revenue reported at **$95.1M**, up 32% YoY.[1]
- Q3 2025 CTV spend contribution reported at **46%** of total ad spend and **45% YoY** CTV growth.[1]
- Viant provided FY2025 revenue and adjusted EBITDA guidance updates in Q3 release.[1]

Public SEC filing continuity (Form 10-Q) supports baseline confidence in reported financial direction and governance quality.[6]

## 7) Competitive Assessment vs MNTN and Tatari
### Where Viant appears stronger
- Public-company scale and disclosure discipline.
- Omnichannel DSP context can attract advertisers seeking consolidated cross-channel execution.
- Explicit supply-path quality investments via direct-access integrations.[3]

### Where MNTN/Tatari may challenge Viant
- MNTN can win where performance-TV specialization and creative automation simplicity are decisive.[4]
- Tatari can win where convergent TV depth and direct-buying workflow narrative are prioritized.[5]

### Likely head-to-head outcomes (inference)
- **Agency and enterprise omnichannel teams:** Viant likely competitive/strong.
- **SMB and highly focused performance-TV buyers:** MNTN/Vibe may be harder competition.
- **Convergent TV specialists:** Tatari may outperform in channel-unification-centric evaluations.

## 8) 12–24 Month Outlook and Predictions
1. **Prediction 1 (testable):** Viant’s CTV share of platform spend remains structurally high, with continued double-digit growth if CTV demand momentum persists.  
   Validation signal: quarterly CTV mix and growth commentary.[1]

2. **Prediction 2 (testable):** Viant expands direct-access and quality-focused supply-path initiatives to improve measurable outcomes and reduce path inefficiency.  
   Validation signal: additional direct integrations and publisher relationships.[3]

3. **Prediction 3 (testable):** ViantAI becomes a larger share of GTM differentiation and may be tied to explicit outcome case studies.  
   Validation signal: product releases and disclosed AI-linked performance evidence.[2]

## 9) Known Unknowns
- Limited publicly disclosed win/loss rates versus MNTN/Tatari by segment.
- Limited detailed public disclosure on CTV take-rate economics by inventory class.
- Limited standardized incrementality comparison against competing CTV platforms.
- Unclear cohort retention dynamics in CTV-heavy customer groups.
- FY2025 final audited annual snapshot (10-K) details should be monitored as filed.
